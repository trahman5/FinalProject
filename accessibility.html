Welcome to the Accessibility webpage

On this webpage, I will cover the fundamentals of Accessibility. And how they actually benefit people with disabilities who use the internet on a daily basis, while also providing insight into how my experience with Accessibility has been. Accessibility is actually both useful and helpful in many ways. For starters, having a webpage with accessibility allows people who have disabilities, whether it is visual, cognitive, motor, or auditory, to experience the webpage like everybody else without having to face any disadvantages. This makes it fair, as it allows you to be able to enjoy the experience of websites without any stress. My experience implementing accessibility has been hectic, but I managed to slowly understand how to implement features such as contrast colors, alternative text, and structural elements, and much more. I believe it is amazing since everybody can be able to enjoy exploring the internet freely and fairly.

What I learned from Web Accessibility

Here are some important lessons that I have to learn when designing accessible websites:

  • When it comes to headers, always use them logically (h1,h2,h3, etc.)
  • Provide alternative text for images
  • Make sure the color contrast in a webpage is readable and acceptable
  • Make sure the texts are big enough for it to be readable
  • Ensure that the links inside webpages are recognizeable and are sufficient
